# H100

**H100** is NVIDIA's Hopper architecture datacenter GPU, released in 2022. It introduced the Transformer Engine with native FP8 support, delivering ~6x the AI training throughput of the previous-generation [[A100]]. The H100 is the current mainstream choice for large-scale LLM training and high-throughput inference, sitting between the A100 (Ampere) and newer [[H200]]/[[B200]] (Blackwell) generations.

---

## ⚙️ Overview

The H100 targets AI training, inference, and HPC workloads. Its key innovations include:

- **Transformer Engine:** Hardware acceleration for transformer models with automatic FP8/FP16 mixed precision
- **4th Gen Tensor Cores:** 3x faster than A100 for AI workloads
- **HBM3 Memory:** Up to 3.35 TB/s bandwidth (SXM variant)
- **NVLink 4.0:** 900 GB/s GPU-to-GPU bandwidth
- **PCIe Gen5:** 128 GB/s host interface

## 🏛️ Architecture Highlights

- **GH100 GPU:** 80B transistors, 4nm TSMC process
- **Transformer Engine:** Automatic mixed-precision (FP8↔FP16) for transformers
- **4th Gen Tensor Cores:** FP8, FP16, BF16, TF32, FP64 support
- **NVLink 4.0:** 18 links × 50 GB/s = 900 GB/s bidirectional
- **NVSwitch:** Enables all-to-all GPU communication in 8-GPU systems
- **PCIe Gen5:** 128 GB/s bidirectional to host

## 🌟 Strengths

- 6x FP16 throughput vs A100 via Transformer Engine
- Native FP8 for efficient training and inference
- NVLink 4.0 enables efficient multi-GPU scaling
- Mature software ecosystem (CUDA 12, PyTorch 2.x)
- Best price/performance for current-gen AI workloads

---

## ⚠️ Weaknesses

- High power consumption (700W SXM, 350W PCIe)
- Expensive ($25-40K per GPU)
- SXM variant requires specialized liquid cooling
- Being superseded by H200/B200 for memory-bound workloads
