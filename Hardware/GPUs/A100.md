# A100

**A100** is NVIDIA's Ampere architecture datacenter GPU, released in 2020. It was the AI training workhorse for years and remains an excellent value in 2025 for inference and budget-conscious training. The A100 introduced 3rd gen Tensor Cores with TF32 and structural sparsity, offering ~2.5x the performance of the previous-generation [[V100]]. It sits between V100 (Volta) and [[H100]] (Hopper) in NVIDIA's datacenter lineup.

---

## ⚙️ Overview

The A100 targets AI training, inference, and HPC workloads. Key innovations include:

- **3rd Gen Tensor Cores:** TF32, BF16, FP64 Tensor Core support
- **Structural Sparsity:** 2x speedup for sparse models
- **HBM2e Memory:** Up to 2.0 TB/s bandwidth (80GB variant)
- **Multi-Instance GPU (MIG):** Partition into 7 isolated instances
- **NVLink 3.0:** 600 GB/s GPU-to-GPU bandwidth
- **PCIe Gen4:** 64 GB/s host interface

## 🏛️ Architecture Highlights

- **GA100 GPU:** 54B transistors, 7nm TSMC process
- **3rd Gen Tensor Cores:** TF32 (10x vs FP32), BF16, FP64 support
- **Structural Sparsity:** 2:4 fine-grained sparsity for 2x throughput
- **Multi-Instance GPU (MIG):** Up to 7 GPU instances for multi-tenant
- **NVLink 3.0:** 12 links × 50 GB/s = 600 GB/s bidirectional
- **NVSwitch 2.0:** Enables all-to-all GPU communication in 8-GPU systems
- **No FP8:** Unlike H100, A100 does not support FP8 precision

## 🌟 Strengths

- Excellent price/performance in 2025 (40-60% cheaper than H100)
- Mature software ecosystem (4+ years of optimization)
- Abundant availability (new and used market)
- MIG enables multi-tenant GPU sharing
- Still capable for 70B model inference (2x80GB)

---

## ⚠️ Weaknesses

- No FP8 support (H100+ only)
- ~50% slower than H100 for inference
- Lower memory bandwidth than Hopper generation
- 40GB variants limited for large models
- Being phased out for new deployments

A100 is NVIDIA's Ampere-based datacenter GPU, offering excellent value in 2025 at 40-60% less than H100.
